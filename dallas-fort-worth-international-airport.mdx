---
title: "Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport"
---

Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) is one of the largest and busiest airports in the world. The airport has multiple terminals with separate highway access points, making terminal confirmation extremely important for both dispatchers and chauffeurs.

---

## Airport Information

| Item | Details | Explanation |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Airport Full Name | Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport | Major international airport serving the Dallas–Fort Worth metropolitan area. |
| Airport Code | DFW | Standard airport code used for reservations, dispatch, and flight tracking. |
| Airport Type | International Airport | Handles large volumes of domestic and international passenger traffic. |
| Location | Dallas–Fort Worth, Texas | Located between Dallas and Fort Worth with access to major business and travel markets. |
| Total Terminals | 5 | DFW operates Terminals A, B, C, D, and E. |
| Terminal Access | Separate highway exit for each terminal | Each terminal has its own dedicated entrance from the airport loop road called International Parkway. |
| International Terminal | Terminal D | Most international arrivals and customs operations occur at Terminal D. |
| Passenger Pickup Location | Arrivals / Lower Level | Passengers are picked up outside baggage claim on the lower arrivals level. |
| Car Service Pickup Area | Ground transportation curb | Limo and black car pickups occur at the designated pickup areas outside each terminal. |
| Terminal Connection | Skylink train system | Skylink connects terminals internally but only after security screening. |
| Traffic Conditions | Moderate to heavy congestion | Wrong terminal routing and airport traffic can create major delays. |
| Terminal Confirmation Importance | Extremely important | Going to the wrong terminal may require completing the full airport loop, adding 15–20 minutes of delay. |

---

## Pickup Instructions

1. Passenger proceeds to baggage claim after landing
2. Passenger exits to the arrivals/lower level
3. Passenger proceeds to the designated ground transportation pickup area
4. Chauffeur meets passenger curbside or inside terminal for meet & greet reservations

---

## Important Notes

- Always confirm the passenger’s exact terminal before dispatching the chauffeur
- Each terminal has its own separate highway entrance and roadway access
- Wrong terminal routing can create significant delays
- Terminal D primarily handles international arrivals
- Pickup occurs on the lower arrivals level outside each terminal
- Skylink train connects terminals internally after security only
- Traffic congestion and long airport driving loops are common during busy periods
