---
title: "Drop-off"
---

Airport drop-off is when the chauffeur transports the customer to the airport departure terminal for their outgoing flight. The ride normally ends after the customer and luggage are unloaded at the departure curb.

---

# Drop-Off Rules

| Rule | Standard Procedure | Why It Matters |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Drop-Off Location | Customers are dropped at the Departures Level (Upper Level). | This level contains airline check-in counters, baggage drop, and TSA/security entrances. |
| Arrivals vs Departures | Lower level is for arrivals only and should not be used for departing passengers. | Going to the wrong level can create delays and customer confusion. |
| Curbside Stop Time | Chauffeurs should stop only briefly, usually 3–5 minutes maximum. | Airport police actively monitor departure curbs and do not allow waiting or parking. |
| Chauffeur Responsibility | Chauffeur assists with luggage and departs promptly after unloading. | Airport departure curbs are active traffic zones, not parking areas. |
| Terminal Confirmation | Correct airline terminal must be confirmed before the ride day. | Wrong terminal drop-offs can cause major delays at large airports. |
| Terminal Verification Importance | Airports like JFK, DFW, and LAX require accurate terminal planning. | Incorrect terminal drop-offs may add 20–30 minutes of additional travel time for passengers. |
| Domestic Flight Arrival Time (No Bags) | Customer should arrive approximately 90 minutes before departure. | Allows enough time for check-in and TSA screening. |
| Domestic Flight Arrival Time (With Bags) | Customer should arrive approximately 2 hours before departure. | Additional time is needed for baggage drop and airport processing. |
| International Flight Arrival Time | Customer should arrive approximately 3 hours before departure. | International flights require additional airline and security processing. |
| Peak Travel Adjustment | Add at least 30 extra minutes during holidays or busy travel periods. | Airport traffic and security delays increase significantly during peak travel times. |
| Pickup Time Planning | Pickup time from customer location should be calculated backwards from flight departure time. | Dispatch must account for airport arrival recommendations, traffic, and route conditions. |
| Wrong Terminal Situations | Most major airports provide terminal connection systems. | Customers may use AirTrain, shuttle buses, or walkways to reach the correct terminal if needed. |
| Common Terminal Connection Systems | AirTrain (JFK/EWR), shuttle buses (LAX/DFW), underground walkways or trains (ATL/ORD). | Understanding airport transfer systems helps dispatchers guide customers properly during terminal errors. |

---

# Important Operational Notes

- Departure curb areas are heavily monitored by airport police
- Chauffeurs should never leave vehicles unattended at departure curbs
- Always confirm airline and terminal before dispatching the ride
- International flights require significantly more airport processing time
- Traffic conditions should always be considered when scheduling pickup times
- Large airports may require extra time for terminal changes or airport loops
